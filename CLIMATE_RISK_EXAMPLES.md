## Example 1: Mortgage Portfolio Physical Risk Assessment

### Scenario
A regional bank has a $5 billion residential mortgage portfolio. Management wants to assess flood risk exposure.

### Step 1: Data Inventory
```
Total Mortgages: 15,000 loans
Average Loan Size: $333,333
Geographic Distribution:
- Coastal Region A: 40% ($2.0B)
- Inland Region B: 35% ($1.75B)
- Mountain Region C: 25% ($1.25B)
```

### Step 2: Risk Identification
Using FEMA flood zone maps and climate projections:

```
Current Flood Risk Exposure:
- High Risk (100-year flood zone): 1,500 loans ($500M)
- Moderate Risk (500-year flood zone): 3,000 loans ($1.0B)
- Low Risk: 10,500 loans ($3.5B)

Projected 2050 Risk (with sea level rise):
- High Risk: 2,250 loans (+50%) ($750M)
- Moderate Risk: 4,500 loans (+50%) ($1.5B)
- Low Risk: 8,250 loans (-21%) ($2.75B)
```

### Step 3: Loss Estimation

**Current Expected Annual Loss:**
```
EAL = Σ (Probability × Severity × Exposure)

High Risk Zone:
EAL = 0.01 × 0.30 × $500M = $1.5M

Moderate Risk Zone:
EAL = 0.002 × 0.20 × $1.0B = $0.4M

Total Current EAL = $1.9M/year
```

**Projected 2050 Expected Annual Loss:**
```
High Risk Zone (increased frequency):
EAL = 0.02 × 0.35 × $750M = $5.25M

Moderate Risk Zone:
EAL = 0.005 × 0.25 × $1.5B = $1.875M

Total Projected EAL = $7.125M/year

Increase: 275% over 30 years
```

### Step 4: Risk Mitigation Strategies

1. **Underwriting Policy Updates:**
   - Require flood insurance for all high-risk properties
   - Apply climate-adjusted LTV ratios (max 70% in high-risk zones)
   - Increase interest rates by 25-50 bps for flood-exposed properties

2. **Portfolio Actions:**
   - Reduce new originations in highest-risk zones
   - Sell $100M of high-risk mortgages to secondary market
   - Target exposure reduction to <5% in extreme risk zones

3. **Client Engagement:**
   - Offer green mortgages for flood-resilient improvements
   - Provide climate risk assessments to borrowers
   - Incentivize property-level adaptation measures

### Expected Impact
```
Revised 2050 EAL (with mitigations):
- 30% reduction in high-risk exposure: $750M → $525M
- 20% reduction in loss severity (through adaptation): 0.35 → 0.28

New EAL = 0.02 × 0.28 × $525M + 0.005 × 0.25 × $1.5B
        = $2.94M + $1.875M = $4.815M/year

Mitigation Benefit: $2.31M/year (32% reduction)
```

## Example 2: Corporate Loan Transition Risk Analysis

### Scenario
A commercial bank has $10 billion in corporate loans. The loan portfolio includes significant exposure to carbon-intensive sectors.

### Portfolio Composition
```
Sector Distribution:
- Oil & Gas Extraction: $2.0B (20%)
- Electric Utilities (Coal): $1.5B (15%)
- Automotive Manufacturing: $1.5B (15%)
- Chemicals: $1.0B (10%)
- Steel Manufacturing: $1.0B (10%)
- Technology: $1.5B (15%)
- Healthcare: $1.0B (10%)
- Other Services: $0.5B (5%)
```

### Step 1: Transition Risk Categorization

**High Transition Risk (HTR) Sectors:**
- Oil & Gas: $2.0B
- Coal Utilities: $1.5B
- High-emission Automotive: $0.9B (60% of auto portfolio)
- Carbon-intensive Chemicals: $0.6B (60% of chemicals)
- Total HTR: $5.0B (50%)

**Moderate Transition Risk (MTR) Sectors:**
- Remaining Automotive: $0.6B
- Remaining Chemicals: $0.4B
- Steel: $1.0B
- Total MTR: $2.0B (20%)

**Low Transition Risk (LTR) Sectors:**
- Technology: $1.5B
- Healthcare: $1.0B
- Services: $0.5B
- Total LTR: $3.0B (30%)

### Step 2: Scenario Analysis (Disorderly Transition - 2030)

**Policy Shock: Carbon Price of $150/ton CO2**

Impact on Borrowers:
```
Oil & Gas Sector:
- Average emissions: 50 MT CO2/year per $100M loan
- Carbon cost: 50 × $150 = $7.5M per $100M exposure
- EBITDA impact: -30% average
- Expected default rate increase: 5% → 15%
- Expected loss: $2.0B × 0.10 × 0.45 = $90M

Coal Utilities:
- Average emissions: 100 MT CO2/year per $100M loan
- Carbon cost: 100 × $150 = $15M per $100M exposure
- EBITDA impact: -60% average
- Expected default rate: 8% → 25%
- Expected loss: $1.5B × 0.17 × 0.50 = $127.5M

Automotive (High-emission):
- EBITDA impact: -25% (demand shift to EVs)
- Expected default rate: 4% → 12%
- Expected loss: $0.9B × 0.08 × 0.40 = $28.8M

Total Scenario Loss: $246.3M
```

### Step 3: Risk Mitigation Plan

**Immediate Actions (0-12 months):**
1. Client engagement for transition plans
2. Enhanced due diligence for HTR renewals
3. Establish sector exposure limits

**Medium-term Actions (1-3 years):**
```
Target Portfolio Mix (2027):
- HTR: $5.0B → $3.0B (-40%)
- MTR: $2.0B → $2.5B (+25%)
- LTR: $3.0B → $4.5B (+50%)
- Total: $10.0B (maintained)

Shift Strategy:
- Natural attrition in HTR: $1.5B
- Active reduction (sales/non-renewal): $0.5B
- New originations in LTR: $1.5B
- Upgrade MTR clients with transition plans: $0.5B
```

**Green Finance Initiative:**
```
New Products:
- Sustainability-linked loans: $1.0B target
- Green bonds: $500M issuance
- Transition finance: $500M facility

Expected Revenue:
- Premium pricing: +0.5% margin = $10M/year
- Advisory fees: $5M/year
- Total new revenue: $15M/year
```

### Expected Outcome
```
Revised 2030 Disorderly Scenario Loss:
HTR exposure: $3.0B (vs $5.0B)
Expected loss: $246.3M × ($3.0B/$5.0B) = $147.8M

Net Benefit: $98.5M loss avoidance
Plus Revenue: $15M × 3 years = $45M
Total Benefit: $143.5M
```

## Example 3: Climate Scenario Analysis

### Scenario Framework
A bank conducts comprehensive scenario analysis across three climate pathways to 2050.

### Scenario 1: Orderly Transition (1.5°C)

**Assumptions:**
- Immediate policy action starting 2025
- Carbon price reaches $200/ton by 2040
- Renewable energy: 80% of generation by 2040
- EV adoption: 90% of new sales by 2035
- Moderate physical climate impacts

**Financial Impacts (2050):**
```
Credit Portfolio Impact:
- HTR sectors: -12% cumulative expected loss rate
- MTR sectors: -5% cumulative expected loss rate  
- LTR sectors: +2% (beneficiaries)

Investment Portfolio:
- Fossil fuel bonds: -40% value
- Renewable energy: +60% value
- Mixed portfolio impact: -8%

Operating Income:
- Net Interest Income: -2% (lower HTR lending)
- Fee Income: +5% (green finance advisory)
- Total Revenue: -0.5%

Net Impact on Capital: -4% vs baseline
```

### Scenario 2: Disorderly Transition (2°C)

**Assumptions:**
- Delayed action until 2030
- Rapid carbon price increase to $300/ton by 2040
- Market disruption and stranded assets
- Higher transition costs
- Increased physical risks

**Financial Impacts (2050):**
```
Credit Portfolio Impact:
- HTR sectors: -25% cumulative expected loss rate
- MTR sectors: -12% cumulative expected loss rate
- LTR sectors: -3% (market disruption)

Investment Portfolio:
- Fossil fuel bonds: -70% value
- High volatility in transition period
- Mixed portfolio impact: -18%

Operating Income:
- Net Interest Income: -8% (defaults and exits)
- Fee Income: +3% (crisis advisory)
- Total Revenue: -6%

Net Impact on Capital: -15% vs baseline
```

### Scenario 3: Hot House World (3-4°C)

**Assumptions:**
- Limited climate action
- Physical risks dominate
- Severe weather events increase 300%
- Chronic impacts: sea level rise, water stress
- Economic growth impacts: -10% global GDP by 2050

**Financial Impacts (2050):**
```
Credit Portfolio Impact:
- Physical risk losses: -20% across vulnerable sectors
- Mortgage portfolio (coastal): -30% value
- Agriculture: -35% expected loss rate
- Overall credit losses: -18%

Investment Portfolio:
- Real estate (coastal): -40% value
- Infrastructure assets: -25% value
- Widespread market impacts: -22%

Operating Income:
- Net Interest Income: -12% (economic slowdown)
- Fee Income: -5% (reduced activity)
- Operating costs: +8% (business continuity)
- Total Revenue: -14%

Net Impact on Capital: -28% vs baseline
```

### Comparative Analysis

```
Summary Table (2050 Impact on Bank Capital):

Scenario              | Probability | Capital Impact | Risk-Adj Impact
---------------------|-------------|----------------|----------------
Orderly (1.5°C)      |     25%     |      -4%       |     -1.0%
Disorderly (2°C)     |     50%     |     -15%       |     -7.5%
Hot House (3-4°C)    |     25%     |     -28%       |     -7.0%
                                                    
Expected Impact (probability-weighted):              -15.5%

Current Capital Ratio: 12.5%
2050 Projected Ratio: 10.6% (12.5% - 1.9%)
Regulatory Minimum: 10.5%

Status: Adequate but close to minimum
Action: Build capital buffer or reduce risk exposure
```

### Strategic Implications

**Capital Planning:**
- Increase capital target by 2% to maintain buffer
- Accelerate transition risk reduction
- Stress test capital plans annually

**Business Strategy:**
- Prioritize Orderly scenario preparedness
- Build resilience for Disorderly scenario
- Maintain contingency plans for Hot House risks

## Example 4: Climate-Adjusted Credit Rating

### Scenario
A bank is evaluating a $200M loan to a coal-fired power plant operator.

### Traditional Credit Assessment

**Financial Metrics (Current):**
```
Revenue: $500M/year
EBITDA: $150M (30% margin)
Debt/EBITDA: 3.5x
Interest Coverage: 6.0x
Traditional Rating: BBB (Investment Grade)
PD: 1.5% (annual)
```

### Climate Risk Adjustment Process

**Step 1: Identify Climate Risks**

Physical Risks:
- Water stress (cooling requirements): Moderate
- Extreme heat (efficiency loss): Low-Moderate

Transition Risks:
- Carbon pricing: HIGH
- Renewable competition: HIGH
- Policy phase-out: HIGH
- Demand shift: MODERATE

**Step 2: Quantify Transition Impact (5-year horizon)**

```
Carbon Price Scenario: $100/ton by 2028

Plant Emissions: 2 million tons CO2/year
Carbon Cost: 2M × $100 = $200M/year

Revenue Projections:
- Electricity demand shift to renewables: -20%
- Pricing pressure: -15%
- New Revenue: $500M × 0.80 × 0.85 = $340M

Cost Projections:
- Operating costs: $350M (current)
- Carbon costs: +$200M
- Total costs: $550M

Climate-Adjusted EBITDA: $340M - $200M = $140M
Wait, recalculating: Revenue $340M - OpEx $350M = -$10M operating loss
Before carbon: $340M - $350M (assuming OpEx scales or includes fuel)

Let me recalculate properly:
Current: Revenue $500M - OpEx $350M = EBITDA $150M
Future: Revenue $340M - OpEx $280M - Carbon $200M = -$140M

Revised EBITDA: -$140M (negative)
Debt Service Capability: Severely impaired
```

**Step 3: Adjust Credit Rating**

```
Climate-Adjusted Metrics (2028):
- EBITDA: -$140M
- Debt/EBITDA: Not meaningful (negative EBITDA)
- Interest Coverage: <1.0x (default risk)
- Climate-Adjusted Rating: CCC (High Default Risk)
- Climate-Adjusted PD: 25% (5-year cumulative)
```

**Step 4: Loan Decision Framework**

```
Expected Loss Comparison:

Traditional Approach:
EL = PD × LGD × EAD
EL = 0.015 × 0.40 × $200M = $1.2M

Climate-Adjusted Approach:
EL = 0.25 × 0.40 × $200M = $20M

Risk Premium Required:
Additional spread: 400 bps
Required pricing: SOFR + 600 bps (vs traditional SOFR + 200 bps)

Recommendation: DECLINE
- Unacceptable risk even at high pricing
- Transition plan insufficient
- Alternative: Require conversion to gas + renewable hybrid
```

### Alternative Financing Structure

**Green Conversion Loan:**
```
Purpose: Convert to 50% natural gas, 50% solar
Amount: $300M (plant conversion)
Timeline: 3 years

Projected Performance (Post-Conversion):
- Revenue: $400M/year (diversified)
- EBITDA: $120M (30% margin)
- Carbon emissions: -60%
- Carbon cost (at $100/ton): $80M/year

Climate-Adjusted Rating: BBB- (Stable)
Climate-Adjusted PD: 3.5%
Pricing: SOFR + 250 bps
Decision: APPROVE with conditions

Conditions:
- Phased disbursement tied to milestones
- Carbon reduction covenants
- Renewable energy capacity targets
- Board climate expertise requirement
```

## Example 5: Green Finance Opportunity Assessment

### Scenario
A bank wants to develop a green financing program and assess the market opportunity.

### Market Analysis

**Target Segments:**
```
1. Renewable Energy Projects
   - Solar farms: $500M opportunity
   - Wind farms: $300M opportunity
   - Energy storage: $200M opportunity

2. Green Real Estate
   - LEED-certified commercial: $800M opportunity
   - Energy-efficient residential: $1,200M opportunity
   - Building retrofits: $400M opportunity

3. Sustainable Transportation
   - EV fleet financing: $300M opportunity
   - Public transit infrastructure: $200M opportunity
   - EV charging stations: $100M opportunity

4. Circular Economy
   - Recycling facilities: $150M opportunity
   - Sustainable manufacturing: $250M opportunity

Total Market Opportunity: $4.4B over 3 years
```

### Product Development

**1. Green Mortgage Program**

```
Features:
- 0.25% rate discount for ENERGY STAR certified homes
- 0.50% discount for net-zero homes
- Higher LTV (up to 95% vs standard 90%)
- Streamlined approval for certified properties

Financial Model:
Target Volume: $400M/year
Avg Loan Size: $400,000
Number of Loans: 1,000/year

Revenue:
- Interest income: $400M × 3.75% = $15M/year
- Origination fees: 1,000 × $2,000 = $2M/year
- Total Revenue: $17M/year

Costs:
- Funding cost: $400M × 2.5% = $10M/year
- Operating expenses: $3M/year
- Default costs: $400M × 0.5% × 40% = $0.8M/year
- Total Costs: $13.8M/year

Net Income: $3.2M/year
ROE: 16% (target equity $20M)
```

**2. Sustainability-Linked Corporate Loans**

```
Structure:
- Interest rate tied to ESG performance metrics
- Rate reduction: 0.25% for achieving targets
- Rate increase: 0.15% for missing targets
- Annual review and adjustment

Target Metrics:
- Carbon emissions reduction: 5%/year
- Renewable energy usage: 30% by year 3
- Waste reduction: 20% by year 2

Financial Model:
Target Portfolio: $1B over 2 years
Avg Facility Size: $50M
Number of Clients: 20

Revenue:
- Base interest: $1B × 4.0% = $40M/year
- Advisory fees: 20 × $500K = $10M (one-time)
- Performance fees: $2M/year
- Total Revenue: $52M/year (year 3)

Costs:
- Funding: $1B × 2.0% = $20M/year
- Operating: $5M/year
- ESG monitoring: $1M/year
- Expected losses: $1B × 1.0% × 40% = $4M/year
- Total Costs: $30M/year

Net Income: $22M/year
ROE: 22% (target equity $100M)
```

**3. Green Bond Issuance**

```
Issuance Plan:
- Year 1: $500M
- Year 2: $750M
- Year 3: $1B

Use of Proceeds:
- Renewable energy loans: 40%
- Green buildings: 35%
- Sustainable transport: 15%
- Other eligible projects: 10%

Investor Benefits:
- ESG investment mandates
- Potential regulatory capital benefits
- Reputational value

Financial Impact:
Funding Cost Advantage: -0.15% vs traditional bonds
Annual Savings: ($500M + $750M + $1B)/3 × 0.15% = $3.4M/year
Plus: Enhanced investor access and diversification

External Review Costs: $200K/year
Net Benefit: $3.2M/year
```

## Key Takeaways from Examples

### For Risk Assessment:
1. Use multiple data sources and scenarios
2. Quantify impacts in financial terms
3. Consider both short-term and long-term horizons
4. Account for correlations and concentrations

### For Decision Making:
1. Integrate climate factors into credit analysis
2. Price for climate risk appropriately
3. Engage clients on transition plans
4. Balance risk and opportunity

### For Strategy:
1. Start with materiality assessment
2. Build capabilities systematically
3. Set clear targets and timelines
4. Monitor and adjust regularly

### For Implementation:
1. Pilot before scaling
2. Leverage external expertise
3. Invest in data and technology
4. Align incentives across organization
